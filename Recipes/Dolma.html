Tasty Dolma with Yogurt

Ingredients:

  • 450g minced lamb or beef, or a mixture of the two
  • 1 medium onion finely chopped
  • ½ cup of round rice
  • ½ cup finely chopped coriander
  • ½ cup finely chopped dill
  • ½ cup finely chopped mint
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • ¼ teaspoon or pepper to taste
  • 80 small or 40 large grape leaves (cut in half)
  • 3 tablespoons of melted butter
  • ½ cup of water

Preparation:

  1. To make the stuffing, knead the meat, onion, rice, coriander, dill, salt and pepper in a bowl with your hands until well mixed.
  2. Prepare the leaf.
    • If using fresh leaves:
      1. Boil water in a pot.
      2. Hold the leaves in portions of boiling water for about 2 minutes. This process is necessary to soften the leaves.
      3. Remove the leaves from the water and drain well.
      4. Cut the stalks.
    • If using salted leaves:
      1. Soak the leaves in water to wash off the salt.
      2. Then cut the stems of the leaves.
      3. Sometimes it happens that leaves placed in salt become hard. In this case, boil the leaves in water for 1-2 minutes to soften them and filter.
      4. If the leaves are large, cut them in half.
  3. Place about 1-2 teaspoons of filling in the center of each leaf. Hold the dolma in one hand and roll the leaf into a ball with the other.
  4. Place the rolled dolmas in the pot close to each other and layer by layer. If you have used fresh leaves, add a little salt between the layers.
  5. Pour the oil and water over the dolmas. Place a small lid or container on top of the last layer so that the dolmas do not open while they are cooking. Put the lid on the pot.
  6. Boil the dolman first. As soon as it boils, reduce the gas and cook the dolman on low heat for about 1 hour and 30 minutes. The inside of the cooked dolman and the leaf should be soft, and the water in the pot should be drained.
  7. Serve dolma with garlic yogurt. To prepare garlic yogurt, mix yogurt with crushed garlic (amount according to taste).

Bon Appetit!
