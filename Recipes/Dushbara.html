Dushbara, or what if dumplings were a soup

Ingredients:

  • 250 g of meat smoked with onions, salted and peppered
  • 2 onions
  • 3 tsp melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon of tomato pulp or 2-3 tomatoes
  • Salt
  • Pepper

For the dough:

  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1/2 tbsp warm water
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon of salt

To make dought:

  • 2-3 tsp flour
  • Dry mint or coriander to taste

To prepare the dough:

  • Sift flour into a bowl.
  • Add eggs, water, and salt.
  • Knead a normal dough.

To shape and fill the dumplings:

  • Divide the dough into two parts and roll each part.
  • Take one of the logs and spread it.
  • Sprinkle a little flour on the rolled dough and spread it thinly.
  • Cut the dough into 2Ã—2 cm wide squares.
  • Place a little meat in each small square.
  • Take one square in your hands and connect the sides from end to end, forming a dumpling.
  • Repeat the process with the remaining dough and meat.

To prepare the broth:

  • Peel the onion and chop it into small pieces.
  • Add oil and salt to a pot and fry the onions until golden.
  • Pour in the tomato pulp and fry for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  • Add water and salt to the pot.
  • Bring the mixture to a boil.
  • Once boiling, add the dumplings to the pot and mix.
  • Cook the dumplings for 5-10 minutes until they are ready.

Final steps:

  • Season the broth with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Sprinkle dry mint or coriander on top.
  • Serve the dushbara with crushed garlic mixed with vinegar on the side for taste.

Bon Appetit!
